## Open Source of the Week

This week’s focus is on the duckh3 project. The **duckh3** is a new open-source R package, developed by **[Adrian Cidre González](https://www.linkedin.com/in/adrian-cidre?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAADEgZz0BYPRpCGBJfzPWzmA_N1cuM6BrNLA)**, **[Egor Kotov](https://www.linkedin.com/in/egorkotov?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAAAKyZSUBhTV1jm_AnJ_Zv4q7JnLLYkwbdvQ)** , and **[Rafael H. M. Pereira](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rafael-h-m-pereira?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAADULxKUBKVSxo8YFCzk-GzXHGpgBMpNWqNs)**, that integrates **[H3 hexagonal spatial indexing](https://h3geo.org/)** into **DuckDB**. It enables fast, scalable geospatial analysis directly within analytical workflows. By combining DuckDB’s high-performance query engine with its spatial and H3 extensions, duckh3 allows users to efficiently convert geographic coordinates into H3 cells, manipulate hierarchical spatial indexes, and work with large geospatial datasets using familiar R tools. The package is designed to bridge database-level geospatial processing with R’s spatial ecosystem (e.g., `sf`), making it easier to build reproducible and performant spatial pipelines.

Project repo: **[https://github.com/Cidree/duckh3](https://github.com/Cidree/duckh3)**

It also has a great hexagon:

### **Key Features**

- Integrates **H3 spatial indexing** directly into DuckDB for scalable geospatial analytics

- Converts **latitude/longitude or spatial objects into H3 cell indexes** at different resolutions

- Supports **hierarchical operations** such as retrieving parent/child cells and resolutions

- Converts H3 indexes into multiple representations, including **polygons, centroids, and vertices** for spatial analysis

- Works seamlessly with **R spatial data structures** (e.g., `sf`) and `dplyr` pipelines

- Enables **memory-efficient processing of large datasets** by leveraging DuckDB’s analytical engine

- Provides utilities to **validate, transform, and explore H3 indexes**, including geometry and indexing properties

More details are available in the project [documentation](https://cidree.github.io/duckh3/).

## Book of the Week

This week’s focus is on a deep learning book: **Math for Deep Learning** by Ronald T. Kneusel. This book offers a focused, practical introduction to the mathematical foundations of deep learning, helping readers move beyond “using libraries” to truly understand how models work. It covers the essential areas—probability, statistics, linear algebra, and calculus, and connects them directly to neural networks, showing how these concepts power training algorithms such as backpropagation and gradient descent. Throughout, it emphasizes intuition and hands-on Python examples to bridge theory and real-world implementation.

### **Topics covered**

- **Probability fundamentals** — rules of probability, joint/marginal distributions, and Bayesian reasoning

- **Statistics for ML** — summarizing data, correlation, hypothesis testing, and evaluating models

- **Linear algebra essentials** — vectors, matrices, eigenvalues, PCA, and SVD for representing and transforming data

- **Calculus and optimization** — derivatives, gradients, and matrix calculus for understanding learning dynamics

- **Neural network mechanics** — how data flows through networks, including CNNs and tensor representations

- **Backpropagation** — step-by-step explanation of how neural networks learn from errors

- **Gradient descent methods** — SGD and advanced optimization techniques used to train deep learning models

This book is ideal for developers, data scientists, and ML practitioners with basic Python knowledge who want to build a solid mathematical understanding of deep learning, especially those who already use ML tools but want to understand what’s happening under the hood.

The book is available for purchase on the **[publisher’s website](https://nostarch.com/math-deep-learning)** and on **[Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Math-Deep-Learning-Understand-Networks-ebook/dp/B096JXMQLM)**.
