"It's the economy, stupid." The token economy went out of control, creating a huge arbitrage opportunity. Why? The math here is simple:

If companies set a daily budget of about $100 per employee for AI usage, and there are on average 220 working days per year, that is a yearly token budget of ~ $20k per employee.

Is spending $20k per employee worth it? Hell yeah! If you are writing code and using AI, you are probably at a productivity multiplier of 3-5 times compared with not using AI.

Are there any alternatives? YES!

Until recently, running an LLM locally was neither efficient nor practical. It required a fancy (and expensive) NVIDIA GPU box under your desk or SSH access to a data center, and your cloud bills at the end of the month were accordingly high. I remember trying to run a model I downloaded from Hugging Face on my M1 machine two years ago, and it was very, very slow.

But a few things changed:
- LLM inference became more efficient and effective thanks to software solutions such as llama.cpp
- LLM architectures improved through methods such as quantization to reduce the model's size

This results in smaller models delivering higher performance and capabilities. The game has shifted from how strong your GPU is to how smart your hardware is and the size of your memory. This is where the Macs come into the picture. The recent MacBook Pro M5 with 128 GB RAM can handle models such as Gemma 4 with 31B parameters and Qwen3.5 with 35B parameters.

So, where is the arbitrage opportunity? Companies will have to choose between spending $20k/year (or more) on tokens and buying a strong MacBook Pro (~$5k) or a powerful Mac Studio (~$9k). When amortized over multiple years, the cost is even lower.

Now, the question is - can open-source models replace proprietary models such as Opus or GPT? Probably not in the short term. But you can definitely offload a lot of routine work to local models and send complex requests to the proprietary models.

I am sure that Uber is not the only company facing this AI budget issue. As companies look for alternatives, it will force Anthropic, OpenAI, etc., to reduce token fees. Therefore, we are going to move to a new equilibrium between using LLMs locally and proprietary ones.

In the long term, open-source models will take over proprietary ones. This is inevitable.
