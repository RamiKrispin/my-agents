# Format: Book of the Week

Heading: `## Book of the Week`. Medium length — between Open Source and Learning.

## Research

- Extract: title, author(s), intended audience, major topics, and the
  publisher/Amazon links.

## Structure (match the examples)

Open with: `This week's focus is on a new book — *{{Title}}* by {{Author}}.`
(Italicize the title. A variant naming the domain is fine: "a new data
engineering book - *{{Title}}* by {{Author}}".)

```markdown
This week's focus is on a new book — *{{Title}}* by {{Author}}. {{1 paragraph:
what the book covers and who it's for — often framed "Instead of {assumption}, it
demonstrates {approach}."}}

### Topics Covered

- **{{topic}}** — {{short description}}
- **{{topic}}** — {{short description}}
- ... (typically 8–10 bullets, each opening with a bolded concept)

This book is ideal for {{audience}} who want to {{goal}}.

The book is available for purchase on the **[publisher's website]({{url}})** and on **[Amazon]({{url}})**.
```

## Length

~120–180 words plus the topic bullets.

## Rules

- Always include the **title and author**; italicize the title.
- Focus on what the reader gains; avoid back-cover marketing language.
- Include both purchase links when available (publisher + Amazon).
